## How Settlements Work

After transactions are completed, Grain batches the funds and settles them to your designated payout account on a regular schedule. By default, settlements occur **daily**, but you can adjust the frequency in **Settings > Payout Preferences**.

Each settlement batch includes all transactions that reached **Completed** status during the settlement window, minus any applicable fees.

## Viewing Settlement History

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Settlements page">
    Click **Settlements** in the left navigation bar. You will see a chronological list of all settlement batches.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review batch details">
    Each row shows the settlement date, total amount, number of transactions included, payout method, and status. Click a row to expand the full details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View included transactions">
    Inside a settlement detail view, the **Transactions** tab lists every transaction that was part of the batch. You can click through to individual transaction details from here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Settlement Statuses

| Status         | Description                                                                                       |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Scheduled**  | The settlement batch has been created and is queued for processing on the next payout cycle.       |
| **Processing** | Funds are being transferred to your payout account. This typically takes 1-2 business days.        |
| **Completed**  | The payout has been delivered to your bank account or crypto wallet.                               |
| **Failed**     | The payout could not be delivered. Check your payout account details in Settings and contact support. |

## Matching Settlements to Transactions

To reconcile a settlement with your internal records:

1. Open the settlement batch and note the **Settlement ID** and total amount.
2. Switch to the **Transactions** tab within the batch to see every included transaction.
3. Cross-reference each transaction's **Customer Reference** with orders in your system.
4. Verify that the sum of individual transaction amounts (minus fees) matches the settlement total.

<Tip>
  Export the settlement's transaction list as a CSV and import it into your accounting software. The **Customer Reference** column maps directly to your order IDs, making automated matching straightforward.
</Tip>

## Reconciliation Tips

<Accordion title="Settlement total does not match expected amount">
  Check whether any fees were deducted. Open the settlement detail view and review the **Fees** section for a line-by-line breakdown. Also verify that no transactions were refunded after the settlement was created.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="A transaction is missing from a settlement batch">
  Confirm the transaction reached **Completed** status before the settlement cutoff time. Transactions that were still **Confirming** at cutoff will roll into the next batch.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Settlement shows as Failed">
  Navigate to **Settings > Payout Preferences** and verify your bank account or wallet details are correct. If everything looks right, contact Grain support with the Settlement ID for further investigation.
</Accordion>

<Note>
  Grain retains settlement records for 18 months. For older records, use the export feature to download and archive your data.
</Note>
