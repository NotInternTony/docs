## Overview

Grain can notify you and your team about important events -- such as completed payments, settlement payouts, and security alerts -- so you stay informed without constantly checking the dashboard. Each team member can customize their own notification preferences.

## Notification Types

### Payment Alerts

- **Payment completed** -- triggered when a transaction reaches **Completed** status
- **Payment failed** -- triggered when a transaction fails or is underpaid
- **Payment expired** -- triggered when a charge expires without receiving payment
- **Refund issued** -- triggered when a refund is processed

### Settlement Alerts

- **Settlement completed** -- triggered when a payout is delivered to your bank or wallet
- **Settlement failed** -- triggered when a payout cannot be processed

### Security Alerts

- **New sign-in detected** -- triggered when a team member signs in from a new device or location
- **API key created or revoked** -- triggered when API credentials are changed
- **Team member added or removed** -- triggered when the roster changes

<Note>
  Security alerts are enabled by default for all Admin users and cannot be fully disabled. This ensures critical security events are always visible to account administrators.
</Note>

## Configuring Your Preferences

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Notification Settings">
    Go to **Settings > Notifications** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose delivery channels">
    For each notification type, select one or more delivery channels:
    - **Email** -- sent to your account email address
    - **Slack** -- posted to a connected Slack channel
    - **Dashboard** -- appears in the notification bell in the top navigation bar
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save Preferences** at the bottom of the page. Changes take effect immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connecting Slack

To receive notifications in Slack:

1. Go to **Settings > Notifications** and click **Connect Slack**.
2. Authorize the Grain app in your Slack workspace.
3. Select the channel where notifications should be posted.
4. Toggle on the specific notification types you want delivered to Slack.

<Tip>
  Create a dedicated Slack channel (e.g., `#grain-payments`) for payment notifications. This keeps alerts organized and makes it easy for your team to monitor activity without cluttering other channels.
</Tip>

## Per-Member Preferences

Each team member manages their own notification settings independently. Admins cannot override another member's preferences, but they can set organization-wide defaults that apply to new team members.

To set defaults for new members:

1. Navigate to **Settings > Notifications > Defaults**.
2. Configure the channels and types that should be enabled by default.
3. Click **Save Defaults**.

New team members will inherit these defaults when they join, and can adjust them afterward.

<Accordion title="Can I receive notifications for only high-value transactions?">
  Yes. Under **Payment Alerts**, click **Advanced** to set a minimum amount threshold. Notifications will only be sent for transactions above that value.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I stop receiving all email notifications?">
  Uncheck **Email** for every notification type on your preferences page. You will still see alerts in the dashboard notification bell unless you disable that channel as well.
</Accordion>
