## Status Page

You can view the real-time operational status of all Grain services at our dedicated status page:

<Card title="Grain Status Page" icon="chart-line" href="https://status.grain.com">
  Visit **status.grain.com** to see current uptime, ongoing incidents, and scheduled maintenance windows.
</Card>

## Monitored Services

Grain continuously monitors the following core services:

### API

The Grain REST API that powers payment creation, refunds, webhooks, and all programmatic interactions. This is the service your integration communicates with directly.

### Dashboard

The web-based merchant dashboard used for managing settings, viewing transactions, generating reports, and accessing support.

### Settlement

The settlement engine responsible for converting cryptocurrency payments and disbursing funds to your configured bank account or wallet. Settlement status covers both on-chain processing and fiat payouts.

<Tip>
  If the API is operational but you are experiencing issues, the problem is likely related to your integration configuration. Check the [FAQ](/support/faq) or [contact support](/support/getting-help).
</Tip>

## Subscribe to Status Updates

Stay informed about incidents and maintenance without having to check the status page manually.

| Method | How to Subscribe |
|--------|------------------|
| **Email** | Visit [status.grain.com](https://status.grain.com) and click **Subscribe to Updates** |
| **RSS** | Add the RSS feed from the status page to your preferred reader |
| **Webhook** | Configure a status webhook in your Dashboard under **Settings > Notifications** |

<Note>
  We recommend that production merchants subscribe to at least one notification channel so you are aware of any service disruptions as soon as they occur.
</Note>

## What to Do During an Outage

If Grain services are experiencing degraded performance or an outage, follow these steps:

1. **Check the status page** at [status.grain.com](https://status.grain.com) for the latest incident updates and estimated resolution times.
2. **Do not retry failed requests in a tight loop.** Use exponential backoff in your integration to avoid adding load to recovering services.
3. **Queue payments locally** if your application supports it, and process them once services are restored.
4. **Review the incident postmortem** once published on the status page. Postmortems include root cause analysis and any actions you may need to take.
5. **Contact support** if you believe your account is specifically affected or if you need help reconciling transactions after the incident.

<Tip>
  Design your integration to handle transient failures gracefully. Our [API Reference](/api-reference) documents recommended retry strategies and idempotency key usage.
</Tip>
