## From Checkout to Settlement

Every Grain payment moves through a series of stages. Understanding these stages helps you build better integrations and provide clearer status updates to your customers.

<Steps>

### Payment Created

When your customer selects crypto as their payment method, Grain creates a payment session. This generates a unique deposit address and displays the amount owed in the customer's chosen cryptocurrency. The payment session has a configurable expiration window (default: 30 minutes) to account for price volatility.

### Customer Pays

The customer sends cryptocurrency from their wallet to the deposit address provided by Grain. This can be from any compatible wallet — a browser extension wallet, a mobile wallet, or a centralized exchange. Grain monitors the deposit address in real time.

### Blockchain Confirmation

Once the transaction is broadcast to the blockchain network, Grain detects it and marks the payment as **pending**. The transaction must reach the required number of block confirmations before it is considered final. Confirmation times vary by network:

| Network    | Typical Confirmation Time |
|------------|--------------------------|
| Ethereum   | ~2 minutes               |
| Solana     | ~5 seconds               |
| Polygon    | ~5 seconds               |
| Base       | ~2 seconds               |
| Bitcoin    | ~30 minutes              |

### Payment Confirmed

After the required confirmations, the payment status updates to **confirmed**. At this point, the funds are secured and the payment is guaranteed. Grain sends a webhook notification (`payment.confirmed`) to your server so you can fulfill the order.

<Tip>
  Use the `payment.confirmed` webhook event to trigger order fulfillment. This is the safest point to consider a payment complete.
</Tip>

### Settlement Initiated

Grain converts the received crypto to your configured settlement currency (e.g., USD, EUR) and initiates a transfer to your bank account. Conversion happens at the market rate at the time of confirmation, so the amount you receive is locked in at this stage.

### Settlement Complete

The funds arrive in your bank account. Settlement timing depends on your settlement schedule and banking provider. Once complete, Grain sends a `settlement.completed` webhook and the transaction is marked as **settled** in your Dashboard.

</Steps>

## Payment Statuses

You can track the status of any payment in the Grain Dashboard or via the API. Here is a summary of all possible statuses:

| Status         | Description                                                  |
|----------------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| `created`      | Payment session generated, awaiting customer transaction     |
| `pending`      | Transaction detected on-chain, awaiting confirmations        |
| `confirmed`    | Payment confirmed and conversion initiated                   |
| `settling`     | Fiat settlement in progress to your bank account             |
| `settled`      | Funds deposited in your bank account                         |
| `expired`      | Customer did not pay within the payment session window       |
| `underpaid`    | Customer sent less than the required amount                  |
| `overpaid`     | Customer sent more than the required amount                  |

<Note>
  For underpaid or overpaid transactions, Grain automatically handles refunds or adjustments based on your account settings. You can configure this behavior in the Dashboard under **Settings > Payments**.
</Note>

## Webhooks

Grain sends webhook events at each major status transition. You can subscribe to these events in your Dashboard or configure them via the API. Key events include:

- `payment.created`
- `payment.pending`
- `payment.confirmed`
- `payment.expired`
- `settlement.initiated`
- `settlement.completed`

See the [API Reference](/api-reference) for full webhook payload details.
